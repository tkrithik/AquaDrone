What Algal Blooms?

Nitrate is a polyatomicc ion, known by the chemical formula NO⁻ ₃. Commonly found in fertilizers and explosives, nitrates can be found naturally in water sources such as rivers and lakes due to bacteria that release nitrates. However, an excess amount of nitrates can seriously injure ones health, such as affecting how blood carries oxygen or causing methemoglobinemia. This excess typically occurs as a result of runoffs from farms with heavy fertilizers, as well as poor man-made water sources. As a team, we cannot sit here and watch as more and more people are harmed by this pollutant. While we can point fingers at farms and other sources of this contaminant, a stronger, wiser choice would be to truly take action against this and develop working solutions in attempt to fight for what we believe should be guaranteed to everyone. By providing technology that can truly save and change lives, our team can make a difference in the world as well.

Reasons Behind Algal Blooms

Farm Runoffs

Fertilizer using nitrates can mix with water sources due to excess ammounts that can be washed away easily.

Sewage Leaks

Natural nitrate forms can be found, such as nitrate producing bacteria. However, these small amounts pose few threats.

Increasing Temperatures

Irrigation systems can act to spread pollutants into water sources, such as pushing chemicas with nitrtes into drains.

Natural Blooms

Poor manmade sources, like wells, can tap into natural nitrate buildups underground, releasing nitrates into water.

Contact

It is always best to contact authorities and report high nitrate concentration sources, for your safety and the safety of others.
